2. Follow-Through Analysis:

  • Follow-Through Bar: After a breakout bar, look for a follow-through bar. If a breakout bar is followed by a bar in the same direction, it increases the probability of the trend continuing. However, if the follow-through is weak or reversed, the breakout might fail. The more consecutive bars in the breakout direction, especially if they close near their extremes (high for bull bars, low for bear bars), the more likely the trend will continue.

 

3. Channel and Flag Patterns:

  • Channels and Flags: View bull and bear channels as flags for potential breakouts in the opposite direction. A bull channel might lead to a bear breakout, and vice versa. Use this to anticipate where breakouts might occur and prepare for entry or exit accordingly.

 

4. Risk Management:

  • Stop Placement: Given the nature of breakouts, stops are usually placed far away, which implies larger risk. Therefore, trade smaller positions to manage risk. The idea is to trade the "I don't care" size, where losing doesn't impact your emotional trading decisions.

For instance, in a bull trend, buying on the close of bull bars, especially if they close near their highs, can be a strategy. Less aggressive traders might wait for a follow-through bar to confirm the move before entering.

 

6. Exit Strategy:

  • Holding for Trends: When entering on a breakout, consider holding the trade as long as the trend premise remains valid, rather than scalping unless you're adept at re-entering trades. The goal is to capture a measured move based on the breakout's magnitude.

 

7. Probability vs. Risk/Reward:
